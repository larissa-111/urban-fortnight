Pochero de Cebu Recipe

About the Dish

Cebu-style pochero is also known as “pocherong Bisaya.” It has a clear, meaty broth and contains a short list of common pantry ingredients. It’s also much easier to cook, which makes it the perfect recipe to try on gloomy, lazy days. This Visayan version is closer to a hearty beef bulalo or nilaga.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg beef shanks
  • 2 pcs Knorr Beef Cubes
  • 2 pcs onions, quartered
  • 6 cups water
  • 2 pcs potatoes, quartered
  • 2 pcs finger chilies
  • ¼ pc pechay, sliced

Instructions for cooking

  1. Prepare the beef shanks: Add shanks into a large pot. Fill with enough water to cover the meat. Blanch over high heat. Once boiling, discard water and set shanks aside.
  2. In another pot, combine Knorr Beef Cubes, beef shanks, onions, and peppercorns. Fill pot with 6 cups water and bring to a boil. Lower heat and simmer until beef is fork-tender.
  3. Simmer until the potatoes are cooked through. Add pechay and simmer until soft.
  4. Serve hot with a side of patis, calamansi, and sliced chilies, if desired.

Note: Make your beef pochero extra nutritious with other add-ons. Baguio beans, corn on the cob, and cabbage are traditional options.
